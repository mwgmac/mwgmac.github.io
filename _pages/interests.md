My philosophical interests are in logic (nonclassical, applications, phil. logic), bits of metaphysics (particularly dialetheic stuff, some phil. religion too), epistemology (esp. modal), phil. lang. ((meta)semantics), and some value theory (wellbeing, properties of moral theories).

Here are some things I'm thinking about:
* Iterated modal ontological arguments. 
	- MOAs tend to have two properties people worry about: the possibility premiss, and strong assumptions about modality (e.g. S5). But there are MOAs whose premisses have iterated modal operators, allowing for a weaker possibility premiss. And you can 'iteratively recapture' some salient modal principles to get K-valid arguments. The iteration is motivated by higher-order properties pertaining to God.
* Metainferential logics
	- These are really exciting. I did my undergrad dissertation on metainferential set theory, and that's something I'd like to think more about. I think these raise serious problems for motivating paraconsistent logics. And they're technically cool.
* Subclassical logics
	- I'm interested in philosophical motivations for infectious and paraconsistent logics, and in the pure and applied semantics for weaker subclassical logics.
